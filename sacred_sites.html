  • SACRED SITES

This is a collection of some of the most revered and awe-inspiring temples of Almora, a picturesque town in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, India. Steeped in history and spirituality, these temples are a testament to the region's rich cultural heritage. From ancient shrines to modern marvels, this website takes you on a journey through the sacred landscapes of Almora, exploring the legends, myths, and stories behind each temple.

Chitai Golu Devta Mandir

Chitai Golu Devta Temple, located in Almora, Uttarakhand, India, is a revered temple dedicated to Lord Golu, an incarnation of Lord Shiva, and is considered the god of justice. Situated in Chitai village, about 9 km from Almora city on the Jageshwar-Dham road, the temple is famous for its thousands of copper bells hung on its premises, which are believed to be a symbol of the devotees' wishes being fulfilled. Devotees visit the temple to seek justice and fulfillment of their wishes, offering prayers, tying bells, and writing their wishes on paper, which are then pinned to the temple walls. The temple is a popular pilgrimage site, attracting thousands of devotees every year, and its serene surroundings and spiritual atmosphere make it a must-visit destination for those seeking peace and solace. Visitors can also explore the nearby markets, which offer a range of local handicrafts and traditional Kumaoni cuisine. The temple's unique blend of natural beauty and spiritual significance makes it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Almora.

Kasar Devi Mandir

Kasar Devi Temple, situated in Almora, Uttarakhand, is a historic temple dedicated to Kasar Devi, a local goddess. This ancient temple is believed to have been built in the 2nd century CE and is known for its stunning architecture and scenic views of the surrounding hills. According to legend, the temple was visited by Swami Vivekananda, a famous Indian philosopher, in 1890, and it is said that he meditated here. The temple's serene atmosphere and picturesque surroundings make it a popular spot for meditation and relaxation. Visitors can enjoy the panoramic views of the Himalayas from the temple's courtyard, and explore the nearby Crank's Ridge, a scenic spot with stunning views of the valley. The temple's unique blend of history, spirituality, and natural beauty makes it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Almora.

Jageshwar Dham

Jageshwar Dham, located in Almora, Uttarakhand, is a sacred Hindu pilgrimage site and one of the 12 Jyotirlingas, dedicated to Lord Shiva. This ancient temple complex, nestled in the picturesque Deodar forest, is believed to have been built between the 7th and 12th centuries CE. The complex comprises over 100 temples, with the main temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known as Nagesh or Jageshwar. According to legend, the temple was built by Adi Shankaracharya, a renowned Indian philosopher, and is said to be the place where he meditated. The temple's serene atmosphere, surrounded by towering Deodar trees and the gentle sound of the Jata Ganga River, makes it an ideal spot for meditation and spiritual rejuvenation. Visitors can explore the various temples, take a dip in the holy river, and enjoy the stunning natural beauty of the surroundings. Jageshwar Dham is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking spiritual enlightenment and a deeper connection with nature.

Vriddha Jageshwar Mandir

Vriddha Jageshwar, located in Almora, Uttarakhand, is an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and is considered to be the older brother of Jageshwar Dham. This temple is situated about 3 km from Jageshwar Dham and is believed to have been built in the 8th century CE. The temple is nestled in a picturesque valley surrounded by towering Deodar trees and offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas. According to legend, Vriddha Jageshwar is the place where Lord Shiva lived in his old age, hence the name "Vriddha" meaning old. The temple's architecture is similar to that of Jageshwar Dham, with intricate carvings and sculptures adorning the walls. The temple complex also houses several other smaller temples, including the Vriddha Jageshwar Temple, the Pushti Devi Temple, and the Kuber Temple. Visitors can explore the temple complex, take in the serene atmosphere, and enjoy the stunning natural beauty of the surroundings. Vriddha Jageshwar is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking spiritual enlightenment and a deeper connection with nature.
